How are the Laws Enforced?

Humanitarian laws are used all the time during wars. A basic
example of this is prohibiting soldiers from firing at doctors or any
vehicles which display a red cross, signaling medics.
Also, holding up a white flag indicates you surrender, and the
combat ends. Unfortunately, there is no proper way to enforce
these laws, so the more specific or obscure laws
regarding the treatment of prisoners can go unenforced.
The most fundamental rules, such as not targeting civilians and
only fighting soldiers, are upheld.

War Crimes

When international humanitarian laws are broken, it is hard
to punish in some cases. However, the punishment for breaking
a humanitarian law is known as a war crime. War crimes today
are taken into the International Criminal Court, or ICC. If someone is convicted of a
war crime, they are usually sentenced to either death or long term imprisonment.
