What is International Humanitarian Law?

When humans go into war, they don't want civilians getting hurt.
International Humanitarian Law is a series of laws put in place
to prevent civilians from getting harmed by the impacts of war.
These human rights laws are an extension of international law,
and are applied by almost every country.

The History of International Humanitarian Law

The International Humanitarian Law first appeared in the 1870s
as the product of work done by various actors pursing different ends.
The actual law was implemented in 1949 in the Geneva Conventions.
Eventhough this law was there, not many people followed it.
Near the end of the 20th century, international lawyers
decided to look into this problem of the human right organizations.
The International Humanitarian Law is not only used in
Genava but it is used everywhere, it is international.

Our Mission

The purpose of War and Humanity is to spread awareness of
the importance of humanitarian laws. Protecting civilians is
essential in war, and not enough people know about these
laws put in place.
